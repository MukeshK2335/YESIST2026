About The Track

The IEEE YESIST12 IEngage Track is a global, industry-driven innovation platform that bridges student innovators with real-world industrial challenges. Aligned with UN SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure, the track empowers participants to develop practical, scalable solutions while gaining hands-on exposure to professional standards and emerging technologies like AI, IoT, Cloud Computing, and Smart Infrastructure.

Participation is facilitated through global direct entries and regional Pilot events. Selected teams receive expert mentorship, compete at the international finale, and gain opportunities for industry collaboration, internships, and long-term partnerships.

Objectives

  • Industry-Driven Innovation: Foster innovation by encouraging participants to develop solutions aligned with real-world industrial challenges and emerging technological needs.
  • Academia-Industry Bridge: Provide students and young professionals with exposure to practical problem-solving, professional standards, and industry expectations.
  • SDG 9 Alignment: Promote innovation aligned with UN SDG 9, focusing on sustainable industrial growth and resilient technological systems.
  • Expert Mentorship: Enable hands-on learning through direct interaction with industry experts and technical mentors throughout the innovation lifecycle.
  • Scalable & Feasible Solutions: Encourage development of impact-driven solutions that can be translated into real-world applications, pilots, or collaborations.
  • Global Visibility: Provide a global platform to showcase ideas, receive expert feedback, and gain international visibility within the IEEE ecosystem.
  • Talent Identification: Connect industries with high-potential innovators for mentorship, internships, and long-term engagement opportunities.
  • Sustainable Ecosystem: Emphasize continuity beyond competition, enabling teams to refine solutions through post-event mentorship and guidance.

Eligibility

  • Undergraduates - Students currently pursuing undergraduate degrees.
  • Postgraduates - Students pursuing master's or doctoral programs.
  • Young Professionals - Early-career professionals seeking industry exposure.

Team Requirements

  • Participants must register as a team.
  • Each team must consist of a minimum of 2 and a maximum of 4 members.
  • Teams may be from the same institution, or multi-institutional collaborative teams.
  • Mandatory Mentor: Faculty, Industry Professional, or IEEE Member.

Why Should You Participate?

  • Beyond Classroom Projects: Work on real industry-defined challenges that go far beyond routine academic or college-level projects.
  • Real Industry Readiness Assessment: Test your skills, ideas, and problem-solving approach in an environment aligned with actual industry expectations.
  • Mentorship from Experts: Selected teams receive structured guidance from experienced industry professionals and domain researchers.
  • Skill Development: Develop hands-on skills in ideation, solution design, feasibility analysis, and professional presentation.
  • Global Competition Exposure: Compete alongside high-performing teams from different countries, institutions, and technical backgrounds.
  • Showcase Internationally: Present your solution at an internationally recognized IEEE event with global visibility.
  • Recognition & Certification: Gain international recognition and certificates associated with a prestigious IEEE innovation challenge.
  • Career Opportunities: Connect with industry leaders and mentors, opening doors to internships and collaborations.
  • Industry-Oriented Experience: Participate in a structured challenge that strengthens your professional profile and resume.
  • Unique Innovation Journey: Be part of an experience that blends competition, mentorship, learning, and real-world impact.

Selection Method

Regardless of the submission pathway (Pilot Entry or Direct Entry), the evaluation and selection process remains uniform. All submissions will be reviewed by a panel of industry experts and IEEE YESIST12 Jury Members based on defined evaluation parameters. Shortlisted teams from both pathways will advance to subsequent selection stages and may be invited to present at the IEEE YESIST12 2026 Grand Finale.

Both entry pathways offer equal opportunity, ensuring participants can confidently submit whether they are at the ideation stage or developing industry-ready solutions.

Track Structure - The Three Pillars

IEngage Track is built on three foundational pillars that empower participants to excel in their professional journey:

Pillar 1: Career Building

We empower young professionals to build a strong foundation for their careers by equipping them with in-demand technical skills and allow them to demonstrate with innovations.

Pillar 2: Industry Skills Development

Embrace continuous learning and develop industry knowledge to stay relevant and competitive in today's fast-paced technological landscape.

Pillar 3: Start-up/Entrepreneurship Development

IEngage provides right guidance with the three stages of startup namely early-stage, venture-funded (growth) stage and late stage. IEngage unlocks your entrepreneurial potential and fuels your confidence for success.

Connect with industry experts who provide personalized guidance and insights to help you navigate your career path successfully.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the IEngage Track?
The IEngage Track is an industry-driven innovation challenge under IEEE YESIST12 that enables participants to work on real-world, industry-defined problem statements with guidance from industry experts.
2. Who can participate in the IEngage Track?
Undergraduate students, postgraduate students, and young professionals are eligible to participate. Teams may include members from one or multiple categories.
3. What is the team size requirement?
Each team must consist of a minimum of 2 members and a maximum of 4 members. Individual participation is not allowed.
4. Is having a mentor mandatory?
Yes. Each team must have a mentor who may be a faculty member, industry professional, or IEEE member.
5. How can teams submit their projects?
Teams can submit their projects through Direct Entry or via Pilot Events conducted by IEEE Sections or partner institutions.
6. Are both submission pathways evaluated differently?
No. All submissions, regardless of the entry pathway, are evaluated using the same criteria by a common jury panel, ensuring fairness and equal opportunity.
7. Do we need to have a fully working prototype to participate?
Not necessarily. Teams may submit solutions at the concept, design, or early prototype stage; however, solutions should demonstrate clear feasibility, scalability, and industry readiness.
8. What kind of problem statements will be released?
Problem Statements/Industry Themes will be released soon - stay observant.
9. What happens if our team is shortlisted?
Shortlisted teams will be invited to present their solutions at the IEEE YESIST12 Grand Finale. Selected teams may also receive post-event industry mentorship.
10. What do participants gain from this track?
Participants gain real-world industry exposure, expert mentorship, international visibility, networking opportunities, and recognition through an IEEE global platform.
11. Is the Grand Finale held online or offline?
All evaluation rounds are conducted online, except for the Grand Finale, which is held offline as part of the IEEE YESIST12 international conference.
12. Will participants receive certificates?
Yes. All eligible participants receive participation certificates, and shortlisted or winning teams receive additional recognition and awards as per YESIST12 guidelines.
